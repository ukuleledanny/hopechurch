Our Doctrine

What We Believe

We are a confessional, evangelical church. These are the core convictions that anchor everything we do.

Scripture

We believe the Bible is the inspired, inerrant Word of God — the final authority for all faith and practice.

God

We believe in one God, eternally existing in three persons: Father, Son, and Holy Spirit.

Jesus Christ

We believe Jesus is fully God and fully man — born of a virgin, crucified for our sins, risen bodily, and coming again.

Salvation

We believe salvation is by grace alone, through faith alone, in Christ alone — and that it transforms every area of life.

The Church

We believe the local church is God's primary instrument for making disciples, caring for souls, and advancing the gospel.

Eternity

We believe in the bodily resurrection of the dead, the final judgment, and the eternal glory of all who trust in Christ.

Our Denomination

The Evangelical Free Church of America

Hope Church is a member congregation of the Evangelical Free Church of America (EFCA) — a fellowship of more than 1,500 autonomous churches united by a shared commitment to the gospel and a common statement of faith.

The EFCA holds to the historic, orthodox Christian faith. We believe in the authority of Scripture, the Trinity, the full deity and humanity of Christ, salvation by grace through faith, the Spirit-filled life, the resurrection, and Christ's return. The EFCA's statement of faith reflects these convictions with care and precision, and it forms the doctrinal foundation of everything we do at Hope.

The "free" in our name reflects a deep conviction about the local church: that congregations should be led by their own elders, shaped by their own communities, and accountable first to God and His Word — not to denominational hierarchy. This is not individualism; it is a commitment to local church health and gospel-centered leadership.
