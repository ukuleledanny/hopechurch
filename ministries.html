Community Life

We're Better Together

The Christian life was never meant to be lived alone. Our ministries exist to connect people, foster discipleship, and extend the love of Christ to our city and the world.

Ages 0 – 5th Grade

Hope Kids

Our children's ministry is a safe, joyful, and gospel-centered environment where kids are taught to love Jesus and one another. Every Sunday during the 10:00 a.m. service, Hope Kids provides age-appropriate worship and Bible lessons for nursery through 5th grade.

We believe that the faith planted in childhood can shape an entire life.

Middle & High School

Hope Youth

Teenagers face a unique set of pressures, questions, and opportunities. Hope Youth exists to walk alongside them — helping students know who they are in Christ, think clearly about faith, and build friendships that last.

We gather weekly for Bible study, games, and honest conversation about real life.

Adults

Adult Discipleship

Growth doesn't stop at graduation. Our adult ministries include Sunday morning classes, men's and women's Bible studies, and quarterly events designed to equip and encourage believers at every stage of life.

Whether you're newly married, raising a family, or navigating retirement — there's a place for you.

Weekly Gatherings

Community Groups

Community Groups are small gatherings of 8–14 people who meet weekly in homes throughout Madison for Bible study, prayer, and life together. This is where the real work of discipleship happens — in the everyday rhythms of shared life.

Groups are organized by neighborhood and life stage.

Local & Global

Missions

We are a sending church. Hope supports missionaries locally, nationally, and internationally — and we regularly organize short-term mission trips for our congregation. We believe every Christian is called to be on mission wherever they live.

From Madison to the ends of the earth, the gospel is moving.

Community Groups

Find Your People

We believe the best kind of church community happens not just on Sunday mornings, but around dinner tables and living rooms throughout the week. Community Groups meet weekly in homes across Madison to study Scripture, pray together, and simply do life together.

Groups typically run 1.5–2 hours and include Bible discussion, prayer, and time to connect. Many groups also serve their neighborhoods together and care for one another in practical ways.
